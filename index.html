Our perspective

Exploring retro aesthetics and early internet culture

We're drawn to the visual language of the 80s and 90s—the constraints that forced creativity. That era had something honest about it. Design that didn't pretend to be perfect, that celebrated its limitations.

There's a rawness to that aesthetic that we think still resonates. It's about intention, not polish. About making something that feels real.

We're exploring how those principles can feel fresh and intentional today. Taking inspiration from the past without nostalgia, building things that feel timeless instead.
